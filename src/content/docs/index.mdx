Every product type, every tax scenario, every shipping configuration, every edge case — how things actually work, and what to do when they don't.

## Part One — Product Types

- **1.1 Simple Products** — One thing, one price, one SKU. The foundation everything else builds on.
- **1.2 Measured & Weight-Based** — Fabric by the yard. Rope by the meter. Coffee by the kilo. Products sold by measurement, not by unit.
- **1.3 Grouped Products** — A display wrapper for related products. No stock logic of its own — each child product manages its own inventory.
- **1.4 Variable Products** — Size, color, material — products with options that each have their own price, SKU, and stock.
- **1.5 External / Affiliate** — Products that live on someone else's site. You display them — they handle the sale.
- **1.6 Downloadable & Virtual** — Files, digital access, and services. Two flags that do different things — and you often need both.
- **1.7 Product Add-Ons** — Custom fields at checkout — engraving text, gift wrapping, color preferences. Flexible. But does not track stock.
- **1.8 Product Bundles** — Kits and sets with real stock tracking. Unlike Add-Ons, inventory actually deducts per component.
- **1.9 Composite Products** — Multi-step product builders. The most powerful product configuration in WooCommerce — and the steepest learning curve.
- **1.10 Subscriptions** — Recurring billing — subscription boxes, SaaS, replenishment, memberships with ongoing fees.
- **1.11 Memberships** — Gated access to content, products, or discounts based on membership plans.
- **1.12 Bookings** — Time-slot and resource-based products. Tours, consultations, equipment rental, classes.
- **1.13 Content Drip & Courses** — Selling access to content that unlocks over time. Courses, lesson sequences, gated libraries.
- **1.14 Print on Demand** — Printful, Printify, Gooten — products that don't exist until someone orders them.
- **1.15 Marketplace & Multi-Vendor** — Multiple vendors selling through your store. The hardest WooCommerce configuration by far.
- **1.16 Stock Management Cheat Sheet** — Which product types track inventory, which don't, and what surprises you.
